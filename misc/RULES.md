The document is a rulebook for the board game "Wayfarers of South Tigris," detailing its gameplay, components, setup, and key concepts.
Game Overview of Wayfarers of the South Tigris

Set during the Abbasid Caliphate, circa 820 AD.
Players explore, map, and manage resources while aiming to impress the Caliph.

Game Components and Setup

Includes 20 Dice, 60 Influence tokens, 62 Upgrade Tiles, 4 Player Markers, 12 Workers, 10 Journal Tiles, 48 Silver, and 48 Provisions.
Setup involves arranging the Main Board, shuffling and placing various Card decks, and distributing resources to players based on turn order.

Setup

Here’s a summary of the setup steps for Wayfarers of the South Tigris:
Main Board Setup

Place the 3 Main Board Sections in the center of the play area (either side can be used for variety).
Shuffle the Townsfolk, Space, Land, Water, and Inspiration Cards into separate decks and place them in their designated spaces on the Main Board.
Draw the top 4 cards from each deck and place them faceup next to their respective draw piles.
Shuffle the Journal Tiles and place one faceup on each empty space of the Journal Track.
Place 1 Green Worker on each indicated space along the Journal Track.
Place all 10 Special (Pink) Upgrade Tiles on their designated spaces on the Main Board.
Place 1 of each unique Green, Black, Yellow, and Blue Upgrade Tile per player on the Main Board. Return extras to the box if playing with fewer than 4 players.
Place Silver and Provisions near the Main Board to form the Main Supply.

Player Setup

Each player receives:

1 Player Board (randomly assigned).
5 Dice in their chosen color (roll 3 and place them next to their Player Board; keep 2 in reserve near the Minarets on the Main Board).
15 Influence tokens in their chosen color.
1 Player Marker in their chosen color (place it on the far-left end of the Journal Track).
1 Yellow Worker and 1 Blue Worker.

Randomly determine the first player. Use the chart to distribute starting Provisions, Silver, and Guild Influence based on turn order.
Return any unused Player Boards, Dice, Influence tokens, Player Markers, and Workers to the box.

Solo Play Setup

Flip one Player Board to the AI side for the opponent.
Place the AI’s Player Marker on the top-left space of their Resource Track and the 0 space of their Comet Track.
The AI starts with 1 Yellow Worker, 1 Blue Worker, and 1 Influence in the Yellow and Blue Guilds.
Shuffle the Scheme Cards into a facedown draw pile for the AI.

Objective of the Game

Players aim to accumulate the most Victory Points (VP) by mapping land, water, and sky.
Points can also be earned through upgrading Caravans, gaining Inspiration, and influencing Guilds.

Gameplay Mechanics Overview

The game is played over an undetermined number of turns, with players taking turns in clockwise order.
On their turn, players can either place a Die, place a Worker, or Rest.

Tags and Influence System

Players collect Tags from various Cards and Upgrade Tiles, which are essential for scoring VP and advancing on the Journal Track.
Influence tokens are used to mark influence on Cards and Guilds, affecting interactions and scoring.

Journaling and Progression

Advancing on the Journal Track is crucial for gaining direction and scoring opportunities.
Players must meet specific requirements to move along the Track, with the end-game triggered when a player reaches the final column.

Guilds and Their Functions

Three Guilds: Science (Black), Trade (Yellow), and Exploration (Blue), each providing unique benefits and scoring opportunities.
Players with the most Influence in a Guild earn 3VP at the end of the game.

Types of Cards and Their Roles

Five types of Cards: Land, Water, Space, Inspiration, and Townsfolk, each contributing to scoring and gameplay mechanics.
Land and Water Cards provide primary Tags, while Space Cards offer various scoring conditions.

Acquiring and Using Cards

Players acquire Cards from faceup options or through specific game effects, with costs varying by Card type.
Townsfolk Cards enhance the usefulness of Land or Water Cards and require specific Tags to be tucked underneath.

Upgrade Tiles and Caravan Customization

Players can gain Upgrade Tiles to enhance their Caravan, which dictates the functionality of Dice placement.
Upgrade Tiles can provide immediate effects, Victory Points, and Tags that contribute to scoring.

Player Turn Actions Explained

Players can place a Die, place a Worker, or Rest during their turn, with specific actions tied to each choice.
Dice placement requires meeting Asset requirements, while Worker placement allows interaction with Cards on the Main Board.

Important Rules and Mechanics

Players cannot activate Card benefits in the same turn they are acquired.
Workers become public resources once placed, and players cannot retrieve the same Worker in a single turn.

Game Mechanics and Player Actions

Players can acquire Comet Cards for 4 Silver, activating immediate Journal effects and Vistas.
Players can Rest if they cannot place a Die or Worker, activating Resting abilities based on the number of Dice in their supply.

Game End Conditions and Final Turns

The game ends when a player reaches one of the five final spaces on the Journal Track.
After the game ends, all players take one final turn, including the player who triggered the end.

Scoring System Overview

Players score points from various categories: Land and Water Tags, Space Cards, Inspiration Cards, Upgrade Tiles, and Guild Majorities.
The player with the highest total VP wins, with tiebreakers based on Black, Yellow, and Blue Influence.

Solo Play Setup and Rules

In solo play, set up the game as a 2-player game, using an AI opponent with specific rules.
The AI does not gain Provisions or Silver but starts with Influence in the Yellow and Blue Guilds.

AI Gameplay and Prioritization

The AI reveals Scheme Cards to determine actions, moving their Marker on the Resource Track based on the Silver Value.
The AI prioritizes actions based on the color of their Resource Track and the sum value of the last two Scheme Cards.

Acquiring Cards and Upgrade Tiles

The AI ignores all icons on acquired Cards and Upgrade Tiles, simply collecting them for end-game scoring.
Upgrade Tiles are placed in a specific order in the Caravan, and the AI resolves any effects immediately upon acquisition.

Here’s a summary of the scoring steps in Wayfarers of the South Tigris:

Primary Land and Water Tags:

Count the number of City, Vista, Harbour, and Open Water Tags (including those in the Caravan).
Score VP based on the number of each type (e.g., 2-7 Tags score 2-16 VP).
Score 5 VP for each set of 4 unique primary Tags (1 of each type).

Space Cards and Achieved Inspiration Cards:

Calculate VP from Space Cards (Sun, Moon, Planet, Comet, and Stars).
Double the VP of Space Cards with achieved Inspiration Card goals.
Check if you have the most Comet Tags for bonus VP.

The Caravan:

Add up all printed VP values on Upgrade Tiles in your Caravan.

Guild Majorities:

Award 3 VP to the player with the most Influence in each Guild (Science, Trade, Exploration). If tied, no VP is awarded.

Determine the Winner:

The player with the highest total VP wins.
If tied, the winner is determined by the most Black Influence, then Yellow Influence, then Blue Influence. If still tied, players share the victory.

SOLO RULES

Here are the detailed solo rules for Wayfarers of the South Tigris:
Setup

Set up the game as you would for a 2-player game, with the following changes:

Choose one Player Board and flip it over to the AI side. This will be your opponent. Each AI Board has its own focus and will feel different to play against.
Do not give the AI any dice. Instead, use the two Player Markers of the unused color:

Place one on the top-left space of their Resource Track.
Place the other on the 0 space of their Comet Track.
Place the Player Marker of their color on the starting space of the Journal Track.

The AI starts with one Yellow Worker and one Blue Worker.
The AI will always be the second player. They do not gain any Provisions or Silver but start with one Influence in the Yellow Guild and one in the Blue Guild. Place their remaining Influence into their supply.

Influence Limits:

Unlike human players, the AI is not limited by the Influence in their supply. If they run out, use Influence of an unused color. This is still considered to be their Influence.

Shuffle all Scheme Cards and place them into a facedown Draw Pile nearby.

Gameplay Overview

Your turns function exactly the same as in the multiplayer game.
On the AI’s turn, you will either reveal a Scheme Card or resolve a Rest action for the AI.
Each Scheme Card will be either Blue or Red in color and have a number of icons:

Silver Value: Indicates how far the AI moves its Marker on the Resource Track.
First Action: The primary action the AI will attempt to resolve.
Second Action: The fallback action if the First Action cannot be resolved.
Special Upgrade Priority and Comet: Indicates priority for acquiring Special Upgrade Tiles and whether the AI gains a Comet.

Scheme Cards

Reveal a Scheme Card:

Draw the top card from the AI Scheme Draw Pile and place it faceup to the right of the Draw Pile and any previously revealed Scheme Cards.
Move the AI’s Marker clockwise around their Resource Track a number of spaces equal to the Scheme Card’s Silver Value. If the value is 0, do not move the Marker.
Resolve any effects along the Track that the Marker moves over (e.g., gaining a Comet, Influencing a Guild, or gaining a Townsfolk Card).

Resolve Actions:

The AI will attempt to resolve the First Action on the Scheme Card. If unable to do so (due to lack of required Influence or Workers), they will resolve the Second Action instead. They will never do both.
If given the option of two Workers, the AI will prioritize placing a Green Worker.

Resting:

If the AI has three faceup Red Scheme Cards or three faceup Blue Scheme Cards, they will Rest on their next turn. Otherwise, they will reveal a new Scheme Card.
The AI will always Rest after revealing between 3-5 Scheme Cards.

Resting
When the AI Rests:

Check the most recently revealed Scheme Card. If it shows a Comet in the bottom-right corner, move their Marker one space up their Comet Track.
The AI will acquire a Space Card, Townsfolk Card, Upgrade Tile, or Influence a Card.
The AI will Journal.

Follow these steps in order. Do not refresh any Cards until the AI has completed their turn.
After finishing their Rest, shuffle all their Scheme Cards back into a facedown Draw Pile.

Special Rules

The AI ignores all costs (other than those on their Scheme Cards) and all requirements on the Journal Track. They still gain all rewards from the Journal Track. In the final column, they gain a Pink Upgrade Tile instead of an Inspiration Card.
The AI ignores all icons on Cards and Upgrade Tiles they acquire (e.g., Comets, Influence, free Upgrades). However, they still gain Victory Points from acquired Upgrade Tiles.
Unless otherwise specified, the AI resolves all icons just as a player would. Some key changes to certain icons are shown along the top of the AI Board.
Any time the AI gains a Comet (never from Space Cards or Upgrade Tiles), move their Marker one space up their Comet Track unless they are already at the top.

AI Prioritizing

Resource Track:

The position of the Marker on the Resource Track dictates a color (Black, Blue, Yellow, or Green) and which Inspiration Card the AI will prioritize (based on the top, middle, and bottom rows of the Resource Track).
The color priority is used for Influencing Cards, acquiring Upgrade Tiles, placing a Worker, or retrieving a Worker. If the color priority results in no benefit (e.g., no remaining Upgrade Tiles of that color), the AI moves to the next color in clockwise order.

Scheme Card Sum Value:

The sum value of the two most recently revealed Scheme Cards (or the only faceup card if there is only one) dictates a value between 0 and 4. This value is used for most of the AI’s actions.
The center-most Card or Upgrade Tile is prioritized if the sum value is 0 or 1. The priority shifts outward as the sum value increases. If the AI cannot interact with the prioritized Card or Tile, they move to the next possible choice, wrapping around if needed.

Workers:

The AI places Green Workers on Townsfolk Cards, Yellow Workers on Land Cards, and Blue Workers on Water Cards. They resolve all printed actions of a space when placing a Worker.
When retrieving Workers, the AI prioritizes Green Workers first. If none are available, they use their Resource Track color priority to determine which color Worker to retrieve. If there are multiple Workers of their priority color, they use their sum value to decide which Worker to retrieve.

Journaling:

The AI’s path on the Journal Track is dictated by the color of all faceup Scheme Cards:

Majority Blue Scheme Cards: AI takes the higher path.
Majority Red Scheme Cards: AI takes the lower path.
Equal number of Blue and Red Scheme Cards: AI follows the color of the most recently revealed Scheme Card.

If only one path is available, the AI takes that path.
In the last column of the Journal Track, the AI will never take the middle option. If the space they would move into is blocked, they move to the other available space in the final column.
Before Journaling, check the AI’s position relative to your Player Marker:

If the AI is behind, they spend one Black Influence to move an extra space if possible.
If the AI is in the same column, this costs two Black Influence.
If the AI is ahead, this costs three Black Influence.
